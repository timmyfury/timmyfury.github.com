Trish and I spent Saturday trekking around New York City. It was a beautiful day and we needed to do something with ourselves rather than sit around all weekend while I wallowed around in the crushing depression that comes along with every birthday. I turned 25 yesterday and its the first time in as-long-as-I-can-remember years that I didn't end up contemplating how to begin my dark memoirs. We saw William Wallace's sword, visited the MoMA, and walked around the city a bunch.

Check out my pictures from the trip on flickr.

Saturday was also my first experience with the New York City subway system. I was really impressed. After spending the day traveling on MTA, with great ease, even with my learning curve, for only seven dollars, it was hard to return home to Philadelphia to our beloved El. I love taking public Transportation. Until my iPod broke, it was my prefered method of travel every Thursday to get to Temple. But, New York is now the third city that has left me pondering the same question. Why do we pay for this? Septa recently tossed around the idea of raising rates again and at the time I was all, "so what." Now I have new feelings on the subject.

In New York, they have clean, well stocked bathrooms. Philadelphia has Bum piss. New York has eager, helpful employees. Philadelphia has sour grapes. New York, Paris and Boston all have expansive underground transportation systems that allow you to get nearly anywhere in the cities that you want to go. Philadelphia has an ass slow bus system with windows so greasy you can't see out of them and piles of chicken bones in the corner.

I like New York. Third trip there in as many years and everytime I like it more. MTA gave me one more reason to love New York and one more reason to forswear Philadelphia. Raise the rates of public transportation? For what?
