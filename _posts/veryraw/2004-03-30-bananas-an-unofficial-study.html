the query

I was at Trish's one morning reading the back of a box of Cheerios Berry Burst with Strawberries and Bananas and there was a fact that I found relatively amazing.

The average American eats about 28 pounds of bananas per years

Good lord man! 28 pounds is a huge amount of bananas. I couldn't get over it. "Thats too much," I say. So after about a week of babbling endlessly about how I couldn't get over how much 28 pounds of bananas was, I decided to take the scientific approach. So follow with me now. I will try to do this in a logical order.

the methods

  1. Bananas at WAWA cost 79 cents a pound.
  2. For five days I bought a banana at WAWA. The prices of these bananas 28 cents, 24 cents, 26 cents, 29 cents and 24 cents. For an average of 26.2 cents per banana
  3. Therefore I conclude that one banana is approxiamately a third of a pound. (79 cents divided by 3 bananas equals 26.3333 cents per banana.)

the findings

My findings were surprising. At 3 bananas per pound, you would only have to eat 84 bananas in one year. Thats only 1 banana every 4.34 days or 1.6 bananas per week. Thats only 7 bananas per month. Thats amazing.

In fact, if you are a 1 banana a day person, in other words you eat 365 bananas per year, you don't consume 28 pounds of bananas per year. You consume 121.6 pounds of bananas per year. Dear lord.
