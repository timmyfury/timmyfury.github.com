Winamp full of Dead Can Dance, Dreamweaver where I am writing this update, my music library, three instant messenger conversations, the websites to two of my online classes, my email, and as if it weren't enough I also have a two perl manuals open on my bed and I have my psyche book in my lap from which I am taking a break to write this. I was sitting at work on Monday and one of my co-workers walks past and asks how I am and I answer what I am supposed to answer, like a good little drone I tell her I am doing fine. Then I realixze that its Julie and I can tell her how I really am. I mean come on its monday freaking morning. How am I supposed to be when I have only had 4 cups of coffee. Regardless, I said, "Actually, I am horribly fine." She likes the the connotation of this and responded, "Well I am excrutiatingly okay with that." We went back and forth for a few moments and came up with a bunch of these. Julie defined them as The Hyperbole followed by The Mediocre. Give em the grandeur, then give em the expected.

I worked Camp-in again this weekend. It was rather fun. I'm really into it this year. I wish I could work more of them. Perhaps a death plot for one of the other three managers. Hmmmm. um... okay... well it was an interesting evening. It was the night of the incident at the library. This wasn't an issue for us although I was worried early in the night that it may get passed to one of the parents and we would have a bunch of histerical mothers on our hands. There were 600, yes 600, girls and mothers in the museum on Saturday night. The only 7 guys there were staff. Damn. Here's the poin though. The craziest part of the night was when I was woken up at 4:20 in the morning and the security guard tells me that there is an alarm clock going off in the store right next to an exhibit that has 77 girls sleeping in it. It took me a good five minutes to figure out what the hell he was talking about. I went down to the exhibit and knew right away I was going to have to call like everyone in the museum at home at 4:30am. This alarm clock was playing some whack ass disco music and singing and shit. It was crazy. I ran around the museum for about 30 minutes finding phone numbers and calling people to try to find a key to the store. No one had one and all the while more and more girls were stirring and my boss was doing all this crazy talk about moving 77 girls to another place to sleep at 4:30 in the morning. At about 4:45am, the alarm had been doing its middle-eastern love cry for about 45 minutes now, it just turned off. And thank god cause I do believe that I had about 10 more minutes before I was forced to walk the plank on the ship in Kid Science.
