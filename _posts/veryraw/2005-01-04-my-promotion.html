So a few days ago I told you about finishing the first leg of my education. The semester was complicated infinitly though by my promotion. Previously I was Library Technology support. After a major shift in the chain of command at the college I was moved into Web Services, given a new title and a little bit of compensation.

I have been studying web standards based design along with server-side languages and database driven web sites for almost two and a half years now. My skill set has grown significantly and I had been looking for a new job that would utilize these skills. I had a difficult time finding anything though because I had no official education and the only proof I had that I could do what I said I could do was my personal sites. And my word... right... personal sites. I have been given the chance to prove myself. It's probably the best thing (professionally) that could have happened to me. When I finished my degree at Temple I'll also have five years of experience in an academic institution and two years in a development environment.

The only thing that makes it better is that I joined the team at a point just before the first redesign of the site in over a year. I was able to influence enough to redesign using web standards. At the moment there are some hitches we are trying to overcome so the new design isn't up. I didn't choose the design, but I am taking the design and marking it up with CSS and xHTML. WOW! How lucky am I?

Plus I get along very well with the rest of the department. We laugh and play and have fun at work. We listen to music and make jokes about goatse. Man, jokes about goatse. (Go ahead Mark. You know you want to click it!)

Cheers to Web Services at DCCC!
