When I was just a wee lad in elementary and secondary school I was more interested in sciences than I was in history. I'm kind of glad I was though because I wouldn't have learned much anyway. I was talking to my nephew yesterday when he found a coin that Trish had brought back from South Korea a few weeks ago. The information that he's missing about the History of the world astounds me.

In school textbooks the history of the United States looks like this...

  • Christopher Columbus discovers North America
  • Pilgrims land on Plymouth rock and befriend the Indians there. The Indians love their new friends.
  • Ben Franklin and George Washington lead the colonies to victory in the Revolutionary war against the Bristish
  • Civil War
  • Abraham Lincoln Dies
  • Great Depression
  • World Wars

Thats it for the History of the United States. Nothing else significant happened in over 500 years of history.

I bring this up because I have been watching alot of History Channel lately. Two shows in particular have been of particular interest. "Conquest of America" and "The Conquerors." These shows have really brought to my attention how many holes there are in my knowledge of US history. Now if I can say this with out sounding arrogant I'll be surprised. If someone as intelligent as I am can be so amiss of information than what is the state of things in the average uninquisitive American's brain?

While I was watching "The Conquerors" last night I saw a parallel between the Conquest of Florida and our present day conquest of Iraq. There are historical similarities where the White house tells the people and the world one thing when they have a completely seperate agenda at hand. Perhaps if more of America knew about the past, they would have made better choices in the polls last November, or at least they would be as smart as Europeans are to question what is going on in the government.

I get the feeling that the lack of real history education in American schools is not exactly unintentional. Although this could be another one of my conspiracy theories. This same sort of thing is causing trouble in China right now over the Japanese leaving their past out of textbooks. I think that when you leave a bunch of stuff out and people question their own government a little less and the somewhat unsavory activities of a government are ignored.

Those who cannot remember the past are condemned to repeat it.
George Santayana
