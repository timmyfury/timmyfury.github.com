I found this wonderful spread in the May 2006 issue of National Geographic. Page 12 features a story of a man from Azerbaijan who was once a school teacher. He was chased from his home from invading Armenians troops. He now works in a salt marsh to support his family. He never sees his children because he has to work so much and his body has decayed from the work he does.

On the facing page, an advertisment for LG. Life's Good. That's some great irony for you. Good job National Geographic.
