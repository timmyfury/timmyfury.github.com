Although I enjoy my job at the college immensely, there was once a job that I enjoyed more. After dropping out of Millersville University, I got a job at USAirways. I threw bags; loaded the bellies of planes. I loved that job like Woah!, but in time realized that someday I would want more then aching knees and back. I left the job and took a less certain path.

I do miss that job, though. I miss racing around on those tugs and conveyor belts. Throwing other people's things around with wanton regard. The excitement as the engines screamed near your head and threw anything in their path across the tarmac. I miss the dominos in the breakroom with guys three times my age. We mocked and taunted each other as we slammed the dominos on the table with vigar and screamed "pants!" at full volume when we threw down some points.

It was the thrill of doing something that others didn't get to do. When the plane was coming in you would watch the monitors as the status updated to "arrived" and your gates team would grab their things and rush out the door to greet the customer. Working hard to get the freight or bags to the baggage claim or to another plane to make a quick connection then loading the next flight's cargo to get the flight out on time.

If you work for an airline on the gates you'll read this and laugh at the romanticized version of what goes on behind the scenes of an airline. It's dirty, hard, honest work, but I loved doing it. To this day everytime I drive behind a diesel truck on a cool morning, I am comforted by the memories of those carefree laid back mornings on the ramp at Philadelphia International.
