A few years ago I started thinking about what would happen if my parents house burnt down and all my childhood images were lost, or worse, all the images of my parents and their parents were lost too. A thourough family history lost to flame. That's rough. That's alot of history. I have commented before that I think I am the single most photographed person on earth. There are literally ten-thousand images of me over the course of my life. My brother and sisters too.

The though of this made me cringe. I enjoy not only leafing through the images but feel that someday these will be the things that allow my children's children to get an idea of who I was and how life was when I was young (that is to say that some wonderful science isn't found to prevent me from becoming a vegetable by eighty years old). So I decided that the best thing to do was to scan every single image in my mother's house (I swear I wasn't high). It's an undertaking that has nearly swallowed me. Sometimes I am really into it and other times I really couldn't care less.

I've scanned images from my father's and mother's childhoods and paintings and poems and handprints from all of my siblings. I've scanned images so old that no one remembers what they are anymore.

The problem then is how the hell do I remember what these images are and who is in them and when they were taken. There's a bounty of information that you might need when you actually want to use these images later. File names and folder structures can only take you so far on this.

I searched and searched for software to help me with the collection of image information. I tried Picassa, it nearly solved the problem but wasn't perfect for what I was looking for. Then I finally tried flickr. As you may already know I am madly in love with flickr. But there is a reason for my childish infatuation which stretches far beyond the desire to share and lookat photos.

When images are uploaded they are tagged (categorized) and dated and captioned and titled. Most importantly though, they are backed up somewhere where the evil life erasing fire can't get it (theoretically).

This still doesn't solve the issue of sitting down at the kitchen table with the kids and leafing through your albums telling stories and laughing like any good hallmark family does, but its just the setup.
