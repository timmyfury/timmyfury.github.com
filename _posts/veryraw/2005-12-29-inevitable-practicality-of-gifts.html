This year was my fourth Christmas with Trish. The first year we were together I bought Trish a gift certificate for sky diving. She bought me something equally exciting. The gifts became increasingly outlandish and wild over the course of the next two and a half years. Nights in fancy hotels, tickets for shows, hot air balloon rides and weekends away were sprinkled in the ranks of gifts.

This was the first Christmas after moving in together. We laughed the other day after opening each others gifts about how practical the presents have become. I gave Trish books and movies and a coat rack. She gave me socks and underwear and a paper shredder. There was an assortment of other gifts but the overarching trend was practicality.

I have been trying to amass a list of signs that you know you are getting old. I think that this goes down on the list. Here is wear I stand.

  • You get angry at a pot roast for not cooking properly.
  • You start carrying savings club cards on your keys.
  • A paper shredder for christmas gets you excited like toys did when you were younger.
