With oil prices pushing ever higher and the impending gloom of peak oil, I wonder how the relative size of American cars will be effected (Check out these photoshoped images of the future of vehicles). The big SUVs and trucks aren't really fun anymore if it costs you too much to drive them. I am hoping these will die a fast death. The same people who drive them are the same people who think that 1 inch of clearance is enough when you are passing a biker at 70 mph.

I want a new car. Not a new used car, a new car. Until now the idea was out of my reach because of my driving history. As of March when my insurance renews again I will be three years without a ticket or an accident and I will turn 26 in April. So my insurance will be lower and buying a new car becomes doable.

If you have had a conversation with me about anything lately you will probably hear me rant about sustainability or conservation or something of the like. I've added a bunch of environment/sustainability related blogs to my daily reading and enjoy getting myself worked up about all the possible doomsday scenerios. Two years ago I would have wanted a really big, fast, roaring vehicle. In fact I sold two such cars a little over a year ago and downsized engine size. At the beginning of the summer I rented a Toyota Corolla while I was vacationing in Florida. Four cylinders of pure joy turned me to the dark side of the economy car. I drove the car nearly 400 miles and at the end of the week when I refilled the tank (the only refill of the week) it cost me 12 dollars. Yeah that's hot.

All these facts combined have pushed me into thinking that I could step down once again and get me a nice four cylinder or a sweet trendy hybrid. If they come out with a hybrid Jeep Wrangler, I am so getting one.
