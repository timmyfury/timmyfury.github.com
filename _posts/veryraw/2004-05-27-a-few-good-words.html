I have been on Dictionary.com's word of the day email list for about eight months now. I don't go to extra lengths to memorize the word I am sent everyday but I occasionally pick them up and stuff them in my daily vocabulary sachel. I was reading through them the other day and I thought that I should offer you all a list of my 10 favorites.

troglodyte
1. A member of a primitive people that lived in caves, dens, or holes; a cave dweller.
2. One who is regarded as reclusive, reactionary, out of date, or brutish.
hullabaloo
A confused noise; uproar; tumult.
ken
1. Perception; understanding; knowledge.
2. The range of vision.
3. View; sight.
flibbertigibbet
A silly, flighty, or scatterbrained person, especially a pert young woman with such qualities.
defenestrate
To throw out of a window.
crabwise
1. Sideways.
2. In a cautiously indirect manner.
bailiwick
A person's specific area of knowledge, authority, interest, skill, or work.
abecedarian
One who is learning the alphabet; hence, a beginner. One engaged in teaching the alphabet.
malapropism
The usually unintentionally humorous misuse of a word, especially by confusion with one of similar sound; also, anexample of such misuse.
neologism
1. A new word or expression.
2. A new use of a word or expression.
3. The use or creation of new words or expressions.
4. (Psychiatry) An invented, meaningless word used by a person with a psychiatric disorder.
5. (Theology) A new view or interpretation of a scripture.

All of these definitions are courtesy of dictionary.com I visit there alot and like I said, I get email from them everyday.
