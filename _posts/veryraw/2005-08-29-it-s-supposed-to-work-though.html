I started school again this morning. This basically means that regular posting will start again because I am going to be thrust back into thinking mode. I don't mind though. As fun as the summer has been I have been stagnating and school always gets the ideas flowing. Regardless, I received an email from Temple this evening that was pimping the beta version of the newest version of the student portal. There are so many errors it makes my heart sad. Here's the one that shows up all over the place.

HTTP/1.1 200 OK Date: Mon, 29 Aug 2005 22:23:35 GMT Server: Oracle-Application-Server-10g/10.1.2.0.0 Oracle-HTTP-Server Connection: close Transfer-Encoding: chunked Content-Type: text/html; charset=iso-8859-1 OK The server encountered an internal error or misconfiguration and was unable to complete your request. Please contact the server administrator, you@your.address and inform them of the time the error occurred, and anything you might have done that may have caused the error. More information about this error may be available in the server error log. Oracle-Application-Server-10g/10.1.2.0.0 Oracle-HTTP-Server Server at tuportal2.temple.edu Port 443

There are more once you log into the portal but for fear the error messages will give something away about my account. I know that Beta webware is all the rage right now but when companies like google or yahoo release beta webware it works. It may not be full featured, but it works. You can't send an email to every student on the first day of class and tell them to check out something cool, then provide them with something broken.

Beside being total bad form, from a strictly development and security standpoint, the information that can be drawn from this kind of message can be used to bring a site to its knees.

On the up side, I did find this page on temple's site that has live webcams. The gears are turning on that one.
