Since Google added satellite images to Google maps and moreso since they released Google Earth I have read a glut of articles about politicians freaking over the ability for terrorists to use the maps to collect information to plan an attack. The idea is rediculous. Maps may be a useful starting place but saying that a terrorist could get the information needed to plot an attack from a low quality satellite image is like saying a research scientist could find everything they need in an encyclopedia. I don't know if they realize it but maps have been available to the general public for decades. I have several maps sitting next to my desk right now. Not only do they have very detailed information about the US and the world but they can also be carried around where ever I go. I don't even need google maps.

The whole idea strikes me as wreaking of two problems. The first is the blatant overuse of the terrorist threat. Its politicians preying on the fears of their citizens. Its politicians making a sad attempt to convince their constituents that they are doing something when most of us know that politicians do nothing but fuel the bureaucracy of the government. Second it shows how little politicians understand about the direction that the future is heading and the impact technology is going to have on it. Google maps has not provided access to previously secret information. Google maps has simply given every person with access to a computer and an internet connection the ability to see it for free. Nothing new here.
