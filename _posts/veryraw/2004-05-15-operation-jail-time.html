Recently Drew at all in the <head> wrote about his ipod and a concept that I think is relatively awesome.

Everywhere you go, you always take the weather with you – according to Crowded House at least. I don’t know about you, but I associate different types of music with different weather conditions. I noticed this particularly the other day when driving along listening to my iPod in the car. It was a really bright sunny morning, and each song selected by the iPod’s shuffle-play seemed to fit perfectly. Chirpy, upbeat music for a chirpy, upbeat morning.

This got me thinking – if I could categorise each song in my music collection with a weather type, all iTunes would need to do is grab a local weather feed in XML from weather.com (just like Dunstan does) – and a customised playlist could be constructed to match the conditions outside.

source: Take the Weather With You

Although I find the concept interesting I don't know if I would use this. I mean its interesting and it certainly presents a challenge from a strictly programmatic point of view but I had another idea which I have thought about for a while. I think adding catagories based on mood would be a more appropriate way of going about catagorizing music. Then when you pick up your iPod or sit down at your computer, you could just pick a mood and the computer would assemble a playlist for you. Songs could fall into multiple mood categories.Would save alot of time and avoid you the annoyance of assembling a playlist everytime you sit down. The problem of course is, as Drew mentions, categorizing 40gig of mp3 is a torture which no man should ever endure.

For a long time I have been thinking about how to approach this task. In the hayday of download, when the cup of Napster runneth over and Audio Galaxy was as extensive as the Milky Way, I used to scrap all of the metadata attached to the songs which I downloaded. Had I just tagged these songs properly when I downloaded them I may not be in the situation I am now. Many gigs later I finally found the program to accomplish this goal.

Call me Sisyphus. I have been working now for almost two months. I am now using MusicBrainz meta-tagger to properly tag my large collection of mp3 which Mark fondly named The Alexandria project (after the Great Library of Alexandria), aka Operation Jail Time, In which we rip our Cd's (using Buzzsaw).

Although this has taken an egregious amount of time, I suspect that the mood tagging would not take nearly as long since there is no research involved, it is merely personal opinion.

All this being said, I will go home and pack my bag and wait for the RIAA to come and get me.
