The following is a conversation, or a paraphrase thereof, which I had at work today with a girl in my lab.

"Do you work here?"

"I do."

"Can you help me?"

"I can."

"I'm having trouble with this laptop. The screen is all widgy on the right side."

"Is it one of the school's laptop's?"

"No."

"I'm sorry I can't help you."

"Please just look at it from far away. Maybe you would have a suggestion for me."

"Okay, no problem but I am not allowed to touch your computer."

"That's fine I just need some advice. My screen is all widgy."

"Define widgy."

"You know, widgy."

"Oh I see." No idea.

She turns the laptop on after struggling with the electrical outlet for about 5 minutes. I look at the screen of this laptop and it is destroyed. It looks like someone hit the thing with a baseball.

"Do you see what I mean?"

"Yeah, it's broken alright."

"Yeah it is. I don't know how it happened."

"Did you by chance... drop it?"

"Yeah."

I stare at her and cock one eyebrow. Scrunching my face slightly like I had just swallowed a gulp of coffee which had been sitting on my desk for a few days.

"So it's not fixable is it?"

"Yeah of course. For the price of a new laptop you could buy a new screen."

So at this point she is pissed off at me because apparently I was supposed to pull out my magic wand and poof make it better.

"Well if I buy a new laptop is there anything that I can do to prevent this from happening?"

A dramatic pause for emphasis...

"...Don't drop your laptop."

It really honestly seemed like common sense to me, but I think at that point she was on the verge of punching me in the stomach. And so it goes another day through in the world of a community college library helpdesk technician.
