If you are like me, you hate adobe acrobat. PDF's are such a flaming hassle that when you see one, you don't even want to deal with it. I would rather just not read some important information than load Acrobat to view a PDF. It is widely accepted that a two page PDF can bring a quad-processor 20,000 ghz computer with 4,000,000 megs of RAM and a 256,000 meg video card to its knees in seconds and leave it begging for it's Mommy.

I recently installed this firefox extension called PDF Download. When you are cruising along the internet and you inadvertantly click a link to a PDF, PDF Download prompts you, do you want to open in a new tab, download to the desktop or cancel. This way you can go on with your day rather than spend the rest of time waiting to regain control of your browser all the while contemplating how awesome Chinese water torture must feel.

All that said, although we have made a very valiant effort to eliminate PDF from the college website altogether, we have been forced to use it in some instances. I recently downloaded acrobat reader 7. What a difference. Load time is down significantly. Seriously like Captain quick launch. It's nearly immediate, with no splash screen. They still haven't done anything about the entire document being redrawn every time you move the document though so it will still lame your computer. You'll just have to wait less time for the laming.
