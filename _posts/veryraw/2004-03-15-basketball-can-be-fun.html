Trish and I went this weekend to see the Harlem Globetrotters. It was so fun. Probably one of the most entertaining things I have seen in a long time. They did the old confetti trick. This is where they pretend to throw a bucket of water on the audience but its full of confetti. I think one of the funniest things was when they were running down the court and they threw the ball to the ref. I could be one of the refs on that show. Mr. Popular, one of the players, came out in the beginning and said, "before we get started, we would like to thank our sponsor, Burger King, for allowing us to eat delicious Whoppers." I thought that was funny.

There was this group of High school age kids that performed a dance thing at half time. It was certainly interesting. There's nothing quite like half clothed high school girls throwing themselves around and rolling on a basketball court. Each one slightly out of step. None of them really knowing what the hell is going on.

The other group that was there was this acrobatic group The Flying Mapapas. They were awesome. They reminded both Trish and I of this acrobat group, The Hebei Acrobatic Troupe of China that performed in the circus last year. They were freaking awesome. They built these huge towers out of themselves. Tres Bueno. Stacking ontop of each other and jumping through each other's legs. I thought they were amazing.
