As mentioned before, I was on vacation last week. Amidst all the sun and fun with the Ellers in Orlando I managed to get through an entire book. I mentioned this fact to Mark the other night in Borders and when I tld him what my fun reading on vacation had been he nearly passed out from laughing.

Electric Universe : The Shocking True Story of Electricity was a great book. I'll give you a minute.

Although I enjoy science, I am not much of a scientific reader. That is, I don't buy books on scientific theory because if the science is too heavy I lose interest too quickly. I read A Brief History of Time but I got more out of Who's Afraid of Schrodinger's Cat. Not because it was better, just because it was written in chunks that were more easily digested. Regardless, this is partially a science book.

It's also a history book. This is the reason I bought it. The delicate weave between history and science is very enjoyable. It reminded me alot of Stiff (which is one of my favorite books all time)(although this one didn't really come close). The best part about this book though was that it had a third layer that history/science books haven't had in the past for me. Electric Universe had personality. I mean personality of the books subjects.

I have always wanted to know more about the figures in my history. I've always wanted to know what these guys were like. Who were their friends and enemies. What did they do for fun. Insecurities? Fears? Ultimate goals and interests. Electric Universe offered that extra piece of the puzzle that I yearn for. Pick it up, read it, enjoy it and thank me later.
