I've written in the past about my fondness of the dollar coin. I find them far more convenient than paper dollars and just think that there is something aesthetically pleasing about the way they feel in your hand and pocket. Dan Platt once said that the reason I like them is that it brings me back to my D&D days, but in real life. He said that I just liked having a pocket full of copper, silver and gold so I could pretend I was going to the tavern to drink a stein of mead before I wen adventuring to clobber a band of bugbears.

However, I enjoy all sort of off-the-norm denominations of money. particularly the two dollar bill. However, I recently read an article on the Washington Post that has made me quite happy. There has been a resurgence in the popularity of one of my favorite denominations. Strip clubs and bars are embracing the bill as a way to increase tipping, but there are other areas that are picking them up as well. The article makes a good observation however about the two dollar bill. In the article, Peter Morici, professor at the Robert H. Smith School of Business at the University of Maryland, is quoted as saying "In order to have a successful $2 bill, you have to have a successful $1 coin." This leads back to my original love for the dollar coin. I was rather happy to find this little bit of legislation, The Presidential $1 Coin Act of 2005. They are going to be minting dollar coins in honor of each of the presidents. Four new coins a year. Looks like there will be more dollar coins soon, so more two dollar bills. Looks like I'm gonna have to find me a bag of holding to carry all this treasure around.
