In my first installment of apartment hunting advice I only shared my observations from the actual hunt. That was in June though, before I have actually moved into my current place. Trish and I did a pretty good job we found a place that we like and we haven't had any major problems. We did however do something right that we had not planned on. This might be something to keep in mind.

We rented a place on the second floor of a three floor building. Because we have someone above and below, we only have two exposed sides to our apartment. We are touched on the other four sides by three apartments and a hallway. I believe the high for the last two weeks has not gotten much above freezing and we haven't turned our heat on yet. We have electric heat in the apartment. Souring energy costs are all the rage on the news this winter and I hear people complaining about really high bills because of heat. We wouldn't know though. There are nights that I have to put on shorts because our apartment is so warm.

My point here is, no matter where your apartment is in the summer, you are going to use your air conditioner. But if you can choose an apartment in the center of a building, you'll save a significant amount of money in the winter.
