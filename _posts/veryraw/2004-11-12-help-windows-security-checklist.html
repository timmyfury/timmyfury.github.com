A few simple steps to a happier, healthier computer!

What are we going to do?

Maintaining your computer is easy. There are a few simple things which you can do to keep everything in proper working order. This list does not require any special computer wizardry. I've received a hundred kajillion request for assistance from people who are frustrated with what their computers are doing. Follow these simple steps and you will find yourself a happy existance which is spyware free, spam free, and ad free. Be patient and don't get discouraged. Good luck.

What do I do?

Run Windows Update

You will need to use Internet Explorer for this. Open Internet Explorer, go to www.windowsupdate.com They will most likely ask you a bunch of questions, for example, 'Do you trust Microsoft?' or 'Do you want to give Microsoft permission to?' I am paraphrasing but you will get the option to click Yes or No; choose Yes. If the options are continue or cancel, choose continue.

Once the page opens you will have two main options, Express Install or Custom Install. Choose Express Install. At this point you will see a green bar moving across the screen. This is Windows Update examining the configuration of your computer. This is probably the only time you ever want a website examining your computer's configuration.

When the results of this search appear you should choose the Install button on the right hand side of the page. You can rest assured that this method is installing all of the most important and sritical updates that Microsoft has released for your version of Windows

Do whatever Windows Update instructs you to do. Be patient. Read the instructions and messages it gives you. This is not difficult. You may have to run this three or four times after restarts to get all of the updates if you have never updated before.

I suggest this be done at least once a week or setting Windows update to Automatically download and install updates. Setting this is easy and can be done from the Windows Update page. This will help you from suffering from horrible flesh eating virii.

Get some anti-Spyware software.

Spyware is software that is installed on your computer without your knowledge. Spyware is angry software that can perform any wide variety of tasks. Spyware can collect what you type. Spyware can collect passwords. Spyware can collect your credit card information, your social security number or worse the addresses of naughty sites you might visit. I'm not telling you this so you leave the internet and never come back. It's important to know about this so you can protect yourself.

Spybot is an excellent free anti-Spyware program. Download this program and run the installation. Very straight forward and easy to use. When you run Spybot, you will need to use the search and destroy feature. It will help you eliminate both spyware and, our next enemy, adware.

Adware. The most annoying part of the internet.

Spam is irritating. The only thing more annoying than spam though is pop-up advertisements. There are several solutions to this plague upon your house. Half of your pop-ups are probably caused by programs called adware which have been installed on your computer in a method similar to the way Spyware is installed. The differences are important though. Adware's only objective is to annoy you with its advertisements. Download Ad-Aware. This is a program which finds the adware programs in your computer and removes them. It also serves as anti-spyware. This program is very easy to use and self explainatory.

The other half of your pop-up problem is surely provided by the pages you visit. If you insist on using Internet Explorer, install the Google Toolbar. It has a built in pop-up blocker and really helps fight this problem. Although if you take my next piece of advice you won't need a search toolbar or a pop-up blocker because thats all integrated into Firefox.

The browser is the proof in the pudding.

A significant portion of the problems that I have heard about on computer has to do with the browser that you are using. If you are using Internet Explorer you are opening yourself to vulnerabilities. Download Firefox. Use Firefox. Never deal with these issues again.

I switched to Firefox about eight months ago and haven't looked back since. I have not had the same problems I had prior to using Firefox and the only explaination is that Firefox is omnipotent. I could tell you about all the wonderful features of Firefox and all the reasons why you should switch but it's already been written. You should read up and then switch. It will save you alot of grief.

If you are using Outlook or Outlook Express for your email you should switch to Thunderbird. It's Firefox's sister email program. Again you wont regret it. Read why you should switch to Thunderbird.

The last bits... virus protection

I start by saying, "I have never had anti-virus software and I have never had a virus." Screen goes blank and Tim screams... KHAAAAAAAN!!! You may want to look into a virus scanning software. My father uses Norton Anti-Virus. The College uses McAfee VirusScan. My father has never steered me wrong but he doesn't have the technical saavy of an entire IT team. You be the judge. Like I said I have never used one but I think they are important.

If you are lookinf for something of the free nature you can download the AVG Anti-Virus program. Not only do they provide free virus protection software but they also ofer free updates.

So what's the bottom line?

So the bottom line is that if you do all of the things I have written about you may still get a bowel wrenching computer virus. You may still have to deal with pop-ups and you may still risk getting hacked. The chance is always there but these will significantly decrease those chances.

When you are surfing the internet use common sense. If something seems to be shady, it probably is. Don't download files from websites you aren't familiar with. Don't accept email from strange email addresses (my email address used to be mulefritters@yahoo.com, that's sort of strange). Don't download attachments from people you don't know. Read the error messages that appear. Don't just click okay or cancel assuming that you are answering the right question. It's better to read a pop-up and answer a question correctly than to assume and end up with a piece of software on your computer that you don't want. You may not know exactly what they mean but usually if you give it that good old college try and use your intuition you will be able to tell whether it's good or bad. Good Luck and happy browsing.
