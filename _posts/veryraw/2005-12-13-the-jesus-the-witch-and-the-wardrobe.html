I knew going into this movie that there was some layer of Christian symbolism to it. I had heard on NPR that it was the conservative right's response to Lord of the Rings and Harry Potter. Furthermore, I have read The Screwtape Letters by C.S. Lewis and know a little about him; beside the fact that a significant portion of the books written from 1800 through 1960 has some level of religious symbolism. That aside, the film The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe had a rediculous amount of religious symbolism. I don't even think I can call it symbolism though. Symbolism is generally subtle. Its implied not forced. The imagery in The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe was so obnoxious that it ruined the movie for me.

A few examples for you...

  • the betrayal of Aslan by Edmund
  • the insuing forgiving of Edmund
  • Aslan's ultimate sacrifice
  • the rediculous unexplained resurrection that followed
  • the bizarre appearance of Santa Clause handing out Christmas presents that had nothing to offer to the story what-so-ever. He magically knew exactly what the three kids would need to win the lame battle at the end.

My other complaint is the complete lack of realism in the battle. No blood, no gore? It's a battle for god's sake. Give me something.

The cincher was at the end though. The kids are all grown up in Narnia-time and as they ride into view in the forest Peter looks like the Burger King.
