Acronyms, particularly technology related acronyms should be used with great care. Using technology related acronyms have a few possible effects. I've made this useful table for you to tell whether you should use them or not based on the knowledge level of the user and recipient and the effect it has.

User knows the acronym User does not know the acronym
Recipient does not know the acronym User totally looks like a pretentious asshole. Recipient is not impressed User looks like a pretentious asshole. Recipient is not impressed
Recipient knows the acronym Good conversation may ensue. Neither is impress by knowledge of acronyms used rather by insight into particular topic User looks like a complete moron and a fake to boot.

I feel similarly about acronyms as I do about the terms information superhighway and computer guy. If you don't know what they mean, they aren't for you to use. As you can see by my handy chart above, in no case are acronyms impressive. The only person that has ever been impressed with acronyms is your mother and thats not because it makes you look smart. Your mother was also proud when you started going poopee in the potty.
